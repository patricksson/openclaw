Why the numbers look so big

Most small business owners underestimate their losses because they only count complaints — the person who rang up angry because you didn't reply. You don't count the people who just silently moved on. Those are the majority.

Here's the breakdown of what actually happens when someone sends you a WhatsApp message and you don't reply within 10 minutes:

  • ~35% message a competitor within the hour. Never come back.
  • ~15% self-serve (watch a YouTube tutorial, buy off Amazon, forget about it).
  • ~20% do eventually reply when you get back to them, but are now price-shopping, so your close rate on these is roughly halved.
  • ~30% never respond when you finally reply hours later. Silent loss.

Combine all of that and the typical "slow reply penalty" lands between 30% and 60% of inbound leads. That's what the calculator's loss slider represents.

Is an AI chatbot actually worth it?

Why 85% recovery, not 100%?

Nobody recovers 100% of lost leads. Some people are going to ghost no matter what. Some messages are spam. Some inquiries are from people who were never going to buy in the first place.

Our pilot measured 85.3% average recovery across the 42 businesses — the calculator rounds to 85% to stay conservative. The AI replies in under 2 seconds, answers their question with your real prices and availability, and in most cases books them there and then. But you won't convert everyone, and any tool that claims 100% is lying.

How the AI actually recovers the lead

The mechanism is simple but the execution matters:

  1. Customer messages your WhatsApp Business number at 9pm on a Sunday.
  2. Automatyn replies in ~2 seconds with a personalised greeting.

The customer experience is indistinguishable from having a human replying — the AI knows your business because you configured it (or we did). That's why recovery rates are so high.

The methodology, in case you want to check my maths
