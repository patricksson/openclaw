1. ManyChat

Price: Free for Instagram and Facebook Messenger. WhatsApp requires the Pro plan at $15/month for up to 500 contacts. AI features (ManyChat AI) cost an additional $29/month or more.

What it does well: ManyChat is the most popular chatbot builder for social media marketing. The visual flow builder is intuitive once you learn it. You can create complex conversation paths with conditional logic, A/B testing, and multi-channel sequences that start on Instagram and continue on WhatsApp. If you are already using ManyChat for Instagram or Messenger, adding WhatsApp is straightforward.

Where it falls short for small business: ManyChat is built for marketing funnels, not customer service. If you mainly need a bot that answers questions, books appointments, and handles after-hours messages, the flow builder is overkill. You end up spending hours building conversation trees when you just want the bot to respond intelligently. The AI add-on helps but costs extra. Also, the $15/month Pro plan caps at 500 contacts. Once you pass that, the price jumps to $25, $45, or more based on contact count.

Verdict: Great if you are running marketing campaigns across Instagram, Facebook, and WhatsApp. Overkill if you just need a WhatsApp bot that answers customer questions.

2. Tidio

Price: Free plan covers web chat only. WhatsApp integration starts at $29/month (Communicator plan). AI chatbot features (Tidio AI) start at $39/month.

What it does well: Tidio started as a web chat widget and expanded into WhatsApp, Messenger, and email. The interface is clean and easy to use. Their AI chatbot (called Lyro) can be trained on your website content and FAQ pages, which means less manual configuration. If you already have a website with detailed service and pricing pages, Lyro can pull answers from there automatically.

Where it falls short for small business: The pricing tiers are confusing. The free plan only covers web chat. To get WhatsApp, you need Communicator at $29/month. To get AI, you need Tidio AI at $39/month. To get both on WhatsApp with AI, you might need both plans stacked, which puts you at $68/month or more. For a small business handling a few dozen WhatsApp messages per day, that adds up fast. Also, their WhatsApp integration is newer than their web chat, so it has fewer features and occasional rough edges.

Verdict: Good if you want one platform for website chat plus WhatsApp. The AI training from your website is genuinely useful. But the price climbs quickly when you stack the features you actually need.

3. Chatfuel

Price: $19.99/month for the Business plan (includes WhatsApp and AI). No permanent free plan, but they offer a trial period.

What it does well: Chatfuel was one of the first chatbot builders and has years of refinement behind it. Their WhatsApp integration includes AI-powered conversations out of the box at the base price, which is rare. The flow builder is solid, and they have good templates for common use cases like lead qualification, appointment booking, and e-commerce support. Chatfuel also handles the WhatsApp Business API signup process for you, which removes one of the biggest setup hurdles.

Where it falls short for small business: No free plan means you are paying from day one. The trial gives you a taste, but you need to commit to $19.99/month to keep things running. The flow builder, while capable, still requires you to think through and build conversation paths manually. If your business is simple (answer questions, book appointments, send hours), building flows feels like unnecessary work. Also, their customer support has mixed reviews from small business users who report slow response times.

Verdict: Solid middle-ground option. AI included at a reasonable price. But you still need to build the conversation flows yourself, and there is no free tier to test with real customers before committing.

4. WATI

What it does well: WATI is built specifically for WhatsApp, which means every feature is designed around WhatsApp's rules and limitations. The shared team inbox is excellent for businesses where multiple people handle customer messages. You can assign conversations, add internal notes, and track response times. Their broadcast messaging and template management tools are the best in this list. If you have a team of 3 or more people handling WhatsApp support, WATI makes coordination much easier.

Where it falls short for small business: At $39/month minimum, WATI is the most expensive starting point on this list. And that base plan does not include AI conversations. You get rule-based automation (if customer says X, reply Y) but not intelligent AI responses. For a solo business owner or a team of two, the shared inbox features you are paying for go unused. The setup is straightforward but the platform has more features than most small businesses need, which can feel overwhelming.

Verdict: The best option if you have a team handling WhatsApp support and need a shared inbox. Too expensive and too complex for solo operators or very small teams.

The bot can only respond based on exact keyword matches and pre-built flows, which means it cannot handle questions phrased in unexpected ways. AI features require paid plans. The platform is also less polished than ManyChat or WATI. Some users report bugs in the flow builder and occasional delays in message delivery. Customer support is responsive but the knowledge base is thin compared to more established platforms.

Verdict: Good for testing the waters with zero budget. The free tier is real and usable. But the bot quality is basic without AI, and the platform feels less mature than the competition.

What to Watch Out for When Choosing a WhatsApp Bot

A few things that are not obvious from pricing pages:

  • Contact limits vs conversation limits. Some platforms charge by contacts (total people in your database) while others charge by conversations (messages exchanged in a 24-hour window). A contact-based model gets expensive fast if you have a large customer base even if most of them rarely message you.
  • WhatsApp API markup. Some platforms add their own fee on top of Meta's conversation charges. Ask specifically whether the platform charges a per-message fee or only the subscription.
  • "AI included" does not mean unlimited AI. Most platforms cap AI conversations or charge per AI interaction. Read the fine print on what "included" actually means in terms of volume.
  • Template approval delays. Every platform handles WhatsApp message template submissions differently. Some auto-approve quickly, others require manual review that can delay your launch by days.
  • Data ownership. Make sure you can export your customer conversation data if you switch platforms. Some platforms make migration deliberately difficult.

How much does a WhatsApp bot cost per month?

From $0 (Automatyn free tier, BotPenguin free plan) to $150+ for premium plans with all features. Most small businesses spend $15 to $99 per month. On top of platform fees, WhatsApp charges $0 to $0.15 per conversation through the Business API.

Yes. Any bot beyond the basic auto reply requires the WhatsApp Business API. Most chatbot platforms handle the API setup during onboarding, so you do not need to deal with it separately.
