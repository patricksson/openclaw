WhatsApp Auto Reply for Business: How to Set It Up Without Any Tech Skills (2026)

You are losing customers every time you take more than five minutes to reply. Here is how to set up WhatsApp auto reply for your business, from the free basic option to AI bots that actually hold real conversations.

It is 9:47pm on a Tuesday. A potential customer just sent you a WhatsApp message asking about your prices. You are eating dinner with your family. You will not see the message until tomorrow morning. The Problem: What Happens When You Miss WhatsApp Messages

Before we get into the how, let us be honest about the cost of not having auto reply set up. Most business owners underestimate it.

When a customer messages your business and gets no response for hours, three things happen:

  • They message your competitor. WhatsApp makes it incredibly easy to search for and message other businesses. Your silence is their invitation to look elsewhere.
  • They assume you are unprofessional. In 2026, an unanswered business message signals that you either do not care or cannot handle the volume. Neither impression helps you.
  • You lose the context. Even if you reply the next morning, the customer's urgency has faded. They were ready to buy at 9:47pm. By 9am, they are back to "just browsing."

One salon owner we spoke with estimated she was losing 8 to 12 potential bookings per week because messages came in after hours and she did not respond until the next day. At an average of $65 per appointment, that is $520 to $780 per week walking out the door. Auto reply would not have captured all of those, but even getting half of them back changes the math completely.

Option 1: The Free WhatsApp Business App Auto Reply

The WhatsApp Business App is free and available on iOS and Android. It includes two built-in auto reply features that every business should turn on immediately.

Away Message. This sends an automatic reply when someone messages you outside your business hours or whenever you toggle it on manually. You write the message, set the schedule, and it goes out automatically. Takes about two minutes to configure.

To set it up: Open WhatsApp Business, go to Settings, then Business Tools, then Away Message. Toggle it on, write your message, and choose when it should send.

Greeting Message. This sends automatically the first time a new customer messages you, or if someone messages you after 14 days of no conversation. It is your digital first impression.

To set it up: Same path, but select Greeting Message instead of Away Message.

Quick Replies. These are not technically auto replies because you still trigger them manually. But they save a lot of time. You create shortcut messages for your most common responses (pricing, hours, directions, booking links), then type a slash command to send them. Instead of typing out your full price list every time, you type "/prices" and the whole message sends.

Here is an example of a good away message:

Hi! Thanks for messaging [Business Name]. We are currently closed but will reply first thing when we open at 9am. If you need to book an appointment, you can do it here: [booking link]. Talk soon!

And here is a bad one:

Thank you for your message. Our office is currently closed. Our business hours are Monday to Friday 9am to 5pm. We will respond to your inquiry during the next business day. For urgent matters, please call our office number.

The first one is warm, helpful, and gives the customer a way to take action right now. The second one sounds like a voicemail greeting from 2008.

The Limitation: Why the Free Auto Reply Falls Short

The WhatsApp Business App auto reply has one massive limitation: it sends the same message to everyone, no matter what they asked.

Customer asks "What are your prices?" They get the away message. Customer asks "Can I book for Saturday at 2pm?" Same away message. Customer says "I need to cancel my appointment." Same away message again.

For many businesses, this is fine as a starting point. Some response is always better than no response. But it creates an obvious problem: the customer still has to wait for a human to actually answer their question. The auto reply just tells them to wait.

This is where things get interesting. Instead of sending the same canned message to every customer, an AI-powered WhatsApp bot reads the incoming message, figures out what the person wants, and replies with the right answer.

Which Option Is Right for Your Business?

Here is a simple way to decide.

Use the free WhatsApp Business App auto reply if:

  • You get fewer than 10 messages per day
  • Most of your messages come during business hours when you can reply quickly

  • You get more than 10 messages per day

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I set up auto reply on WhatsApp Business?

Open the WhatsApp Business App, go to Settings, then Business Tools, then Away Message. Toggle it on, write your message, and choose a schedule. This gives you a basic auto reply. For smarter replies that answer specific questions, you need a WhatsApp Business API chatbot, which platforms like Automatyn set up for you with no technical skills required.

Can WhatsApp auto reply answer different questions automatically?

The free WhatsApp Business App auto reply cannot. It sends the same message to everyone. To get context-aware replies that match each customer's question, you need an AI-powered bot connected through the WhatsApp Business API. These bots understand what the customer is asking and respond with the right answer automatically.

Is WhatsApp auto reply free?

The basic away message and greeting message in the WhatsApp Business App are completely free. AI-powered auto reply through the WhatsApp Business API has costs: conversation fees range from free to $0.15 per message, and chatbot platforms charge $0 to $99 per month. Automatyn offers a free tier that includes basic WhatsApp AI auto reply.

What should I write in my WhatsApp business auto reply message?

Keep it short, warm, and action-oriented. Include your business name, when they can expect a reply, and a way for them to take action right now (like a booking link). Avoid long paragraphs and corporate language. Sound like a person, not a machine.

Can I set different auto replies for different times of day?

The WhatsApp Business App lets you set an away message that triggers only outside business hours. But it cannot send different messages based on the time or the customer's question. For that, you need an API-connected chatbot that can handle conditional logic and route conversations based on context.

Will customers know they are talking to a bot?

With the basic auto reply, yes, it is obviously automated. With a well-configured AI chatbot, most customers do not notice. Modern AI bots reply in natural language, answer specific questions accurately, and handle follow-ups in context. The key is making it sound like a helpful team member, not a menu system.
