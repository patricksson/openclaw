The Three Messages That Do Almost All the Work

You do not need a complicated funnel. Three simple automated messages recover the vast majority of lost revenue. Here is what each one looks like and when it fires.

1. The 24-hour reminder

Fires the day before the booking. Plain, warm, and actionable. Something like:

Hi Sarah, this is a reminder that you have an appointment tomorrow (Thursday) at 2pm with Tara. If you need to change it, just reply here and we will sort it out. See you tomorrow.

Why it works: people book appointments days or weeks in advance and genuinely forget. This message lands 24 hours before, gives them a chance to either confirm mentally or reschedule cleanly. Either way, you avoid an empty slot.

2. The 2-hour reminder

Shorter, more urgent. Fires a couple of hours before the appointment. Something like:

Quick heads-up, Sarah — your 2pm appointment is today. We will see you soon. If anything changes, reply here.

This one catches the people who forgot the 24-hour reminder and were about to ghost. It also gives customers time to let you know if they are running late, so you can adjust instead of sitting there wondering.

3. The no-show rescue message

Fires 15 minutes after the booked time if the customer has not shown up. This is the one that actually recovers revenue.

Hi Sarah, we had you down for 2pm today and were not sure if you are still on your way or if something came up. Happy to reschedule if easier — just reply and I will find you a new slot.

Notice the tone. It is not aggressive. It is not making them feel bad. It is offering an out: "if something came up, let's just rebook." About 30% of people will take that out rather than permanently ghost. The rest quietly vanish, which is the same outcome you would have had anyway. You lose nothing by asking.
