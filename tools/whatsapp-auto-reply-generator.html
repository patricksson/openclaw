How WhatsApp auto-replies work for small businesses

WhatsApp Business gives you two kinds of automatic messages: away messages (sent when you're outside business hours) and greeting messages (sent when someone messages you for the first time, or after 14 days of inactivity).

A well-written auto-reply does three things: sets a concrete time promise ("within 30 minutes"), tells the customer how to self-serve (prices, hours, booking link), and feels human — not robotic.

The weakness of a static auto-reply is that it can't actually answer questions. If a customer asks "do you have availability Thursday at 4pm?", your auto-reply says "we'll get back to you" — and half of them book with someone else in the meantime.

Frequently asked questions

Is WhatsApp Business auto-reply free? +

Yes. WhatsApp Business (the free app) includes away messages and greeting messages at no cost. You download it from the App Store or Google Play.

Can I send different auto-replies to different customers? +

Not with the built-in feature — it sends the same message to everyone. For dynamic, customer-aware replies (answering their specific question, using their name, booking their slot), you need an AI receptionist like Automatyn.

What's the difference between auto-reply and an AI chatbot? +

Auto-reply = one fixed message sent regardless of what the customer asks. AI chatbot = reads the customer's actual message, understands it, and answers with the right information (prices, availability, booking). Automatyn is the second kind — £29/mo, 10-minute setup.

Will auto-replies get my WhatsApp number banned? +

No — auto-replies in WhatsApp Business are official and supported. You only risk bans if you send unsolicited bulk messages (spam) or use unofficial tools that violate WhatsApp's terms. Automatyn uses the official WhatsApp Business API, so it's fully compliant.
